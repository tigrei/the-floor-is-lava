MARITIME WEATHER RESISTANCE FIELDS — DATA DESCRIPTION
======================================================

FILES
  typhoon.json / squall.json / borneo_vortex.json / itcz.json / hectic.json

STRUCTURE
  { "cells": { "x_y": [r0, r1, ... r17] } }

  x        0–49   west → east   (100°E – 125°E)
  y        0–49   south → north (0°N   – 25°N)
  r        0.0–1.0 resistance per 10-second frame
             0.0 = free sailing
             1.0 = impassable

  18 frames, Δt = 10 s, total span = 0–170 s (≈ 3 minutes)

LOOKUP
  resistance = data["cells"]["x_y"][frame_index]

SCENARIOS
  typhoon        Eye enters east edge (~125°E, 14°N), tracks WNW toward Vietnam.
                 Calm eye, violent eyewall (52 m/s). Resistance peaks ~0.96 at eyewall.

  squall         Localised convective cell over Strait of Malacca.
                 Bell-curve lifecycle — forms, peaks at t≈85s, dissipates.
                 Resistance max ~0.71, rest of grid near 0.

  borneo_vortex  Quasi-stationary vortex off NW Borneo under NE monsoon cold surge.
                 Broad moderate resistance 0.27–0.95, slowly intensifying.

  itcz           ITCZ/MJO monsoon trough migrating north (5°N → 10°N).
                 Resistance band sweeps northward over 3 minutes.

  hectic         All systems simultaneously active + turbulent noise.
                 Resistance 0.40–0.96 everywhere, no safe corridor.

PHYSICS
  Resistance = 1 − (wave_penalty × windage_penalty)

  wave_penalty   DNV-GL voluntary speed reduction from significant wave height Hₛ
                 and encounter angle (head/beam/following sea).
                 Hₛ from JONSWAP spectrum (fully-developed sea cap applied).
                 SW Indian Ocean swell (14 s period) superposed independently.

  windage_penalty  Aerodynamic drag on 800 m² superstructure at 6 m/s design speed.

  No bathymetry. No land mask. Pure weather forcing.
